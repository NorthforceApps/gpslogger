Private location history

Private Google Timeline alternative for Android route history

If you want a record of where you have been, but you do not want every trip tied to a cloud profile, GPS Logger gives you a more deliberate model: start a route, save it on your phone, browse it later, and export it whenever you need.

Quick answer

GPS Logger is a private route-history app for Android. It is best when you want user-started GPS recordings, a personal atlas, GPX/KML export and no account. It is not a silent always-on copy of every movement; you decide when recording starts and stops.

Why people look beyond automatic location history

Automatic location history can be convenient, but it often comes with trade-offs: cloud dependence, unclear export paths, missing trips, or a feeling that the record is not fully yours. For many people, the better answer is not recording everything. It is recording the routes that matter and keeping the files portable.

What GPS Logger does differently

  • You start the recording. Walks, hikes, rides, road trips, commutes and field visits become explicit saved tracks.
  • History stays local by default. Saved routes live in the app database on your phone.
  • The archive is visual. Browse a lifetime map, calendar, repeated places, countries and individual route stats.
  • Data is portable. Export individual tracks as GPX or KML, or create a whole-history GPX ZIP backup.
  • Live sharing is separate. When enabled, App Cloud shares current position only, not a server-side trail of saved trips.

When GPS Logger is a good fit

Use it if you want a private travel log, outdoor route journal, commute archive, road-trip memory, site-visit record or GPX collection that you can export. It is especially useful if your goal is remembering and owning route data rather than sharing workouts or building a social profile.

When it is not the right fit

If you want a fully automatic timeline of every shop, train station, cafe and short movement with no manual start, GPS Logger is intentionally more controlled than that. Optional auto-start after reboot helps specific users who want persistent recording, but normal recording is still a visible action you control.

How to migrate old tracks

If you already have GPX files from another app or device, import them into GPS Logger and see them in one atlas. Bulk import accepts multiple GPX files or a ZIP backup, and the app shows any current import limits before you start.

FAQ

Does GPS Logger upload my route history?

Saved tracks stay on your device by default. Data leaves only when you choose a feature that needs it, such as export, App Cloud live sharing or self-hosted live upload.

Can I export everything?

Yes. You can export individual tracks as GPX or KML and create a full-history GPX ZIP backup.

Can I import old GPX files?

Yes. GPS Logger imports GPX files and ZIP archives, then adds the routes to your history and atlas.

Does it work without an account?

Yes. Recording, history, import and export do not require an account.
